# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual biodiversity-supported ecological resilience scoring models.

The scores are not objective ecological truth. They are diagnostic tools for making biodiversity capacity, ecological pressure, resilience gaps, and monitoring needs visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Biodiversity-supported resilience**: estimates how genetic diversity, species diversity, functional diversity, connectivity, ecosystem integrity, adaptive capacity, governance quality, and stewardship support ecological resilience.
- **Ecological pressure**: estimates stress from fragmentation, pollution, invasive species, extraction, climate stress, and monitoring gaps.
- **Ecological resilience gap**: compares biodiversity-supported resilience against ecological pressure.
- **Functional diversity**: represents the range of ecological roles available in the system.
- **Response diversity**: represents variation in how organisms respond to disturbance.
- **Habitat connectivity**: represents the ability of organisms, genes, water, nutrients, and ecological processes to move across landscapes or seascapes.
- **Ecological memory**: represents living and structural legacies that support recovery after disturbance.

## Interpretation

An ecosystem may appear intact while losing resilience if biodiversity, functional diversity, connectivity, or ecological memory decline.

The model is most useful for ecological monitoring, restoration prioritization, biodiversity-sensitive climate adaptation, conservation planning, watershed analysis, food-system resilience, and biodiversity governance review.
