# Biodiversity and Ecological Resilience Review Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing ecological resilience, biodiversity monitoring, restoration planning, climate adaptation, watershed management, protected-area effectiveness, or nature-based resilience strategies.

## Biodiversity Capacity

- Is genetic diversity considered?
- Is species diversity measured or approximated?
- Are functional groups represented?
- Are keystone, foundation, engineer, or culturally important species identified?
- Is response diversity considered under climate and disturbance stress?

## Connectivity and Ecological Memory

- Are habitat corridors mapped?
- Are source populations, seed banks, soil biota, refugia, and recovery pathways identified?
- Are landscape and watershed connections considered?
- Are barriers such as roads, dams, urban expansion, and fragmentation addressed?

## Pressure and Thresholds

- Are fragmentation, pollution, invasive species, extraction, and climate stress tracked?
- Are ecological thresholds or regime-shift risks identified?
- Are cumulative pressures evaluated together?
- Are monitoring gaps visible?

## Governance and Justice

- Are Indigenous and local knowledge systems respected?
- Are land rights, tenure, consent, and stewardship included?
- Are protected-area and restoration strategies socially legitimate?
- Are vulnerable communities protected from conservation-related displacement or exclusion?

## Measurement and Learning

- Are indicators connected to decisions?
- Are uncertainty and data gaps documented?
- Are restoration outcomes monitored over time?
- Are policies updated after evidence changes?
