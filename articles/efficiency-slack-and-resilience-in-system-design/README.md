# Efficiency, Slack, and Resilience in System Design

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Efficiency, Slack, and Resilience in System Design**

The workflows model how routine efficiency, protective slack, redundancy, modularity, diversity, feedback and monitoring, repair capacity, governance quality, tight coupling, single-point dependence, overload, deferred maintenance, and hidden risk transfer shape resilience-aware system performance.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- Efficiency as ordinary-period optimization
- Slack as protective capacity
- Resilience as preserved function under disruption
- Lean systems and hidden fragility
- Supply-chain optimization
- Public-sector surge capacity
- Infrastructure maintenance and reserve capacity
- Energy, water, health, and critical functions
- Redundancy and modularity
- Tight coupling and single points of failure
- Hidden risk transfer to workers, households, suppliers, communities, ecosystems, and future budgets
- Justice in system design
- Resilient efficiency as a sustainable design principle

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
efficiency_slack_resilience_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `routine_efficiency_index`: higher = stronger ordinary-period efficiency
- `protective_slack_index`: higher = stronger buffers and spare capacity
- `tight_coupling_index`: higher = faster failure propagation
- `hidden_risk_transfer_index`: higher = more risk shifted onto less powerful actors
