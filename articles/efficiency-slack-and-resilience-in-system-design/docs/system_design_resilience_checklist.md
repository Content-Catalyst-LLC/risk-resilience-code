# Efficiency, Slack, and Resilience Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing supply chains, critical infrastructure, public services, healthcare systems, energy and water utilities, institutional capacity, or resilience investment strategy.

## Efficiency and Assumptions

- What conditions does the system assume are normal?
- Which efficiency metrics dominate current decision-making?
- Are failure costs included in efficiency calculations?
- Is the system efficient across uncertainty, or only under ideal conditions?

## Protective Slack

- What buffers exist for demand surges, delays, shortages, workforce loss, or component failure?
- Which forms of slack are truly protective?
- Which forms are wasteful or poorly governed?
- Are critical functions protected by enough reserve capacity?

## Fragility Mechanisms

- Where is the system tightly coupled?
- Where are single points of failure?
- What maintenance has been deferred?
- Are workers, households, suppliers, or communities absorbing hidden risk?
- Is chronic overload being mistaken for productivity?

## Resilient Design

- Are redundancy and modularity designed around essential functions?
- Can failure be isolated?
- Can the system reroute, substitute, repair, or decentralize?
- Are monitoring and feedback strong enough to detect stress early?
- Are governance responsibilities clear?

## Justice

- Who benefits from efficiency gains?
- Who bears the consequences when assumptions fail?
- Are buffers available publicly and equitably, or only privately?
- Are worker capacity, community trust, and public legitimacy treated as resilience assets?
