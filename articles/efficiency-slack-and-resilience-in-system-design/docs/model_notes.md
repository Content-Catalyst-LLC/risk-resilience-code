# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual efficiency, slack, and resilience scoring models.

The scores are not objective certifications of system resilience. They are diagnostic tools for making routine efficiency, protective slack, redundancy, modularity, diversity, feedback and monitoring, repair capacity, governance quality, tight coupling, single-point dependence, overload, deferred maintenance, and hidden risk transfer visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Routine efficiency**: ordinary-period cost, throughput, utilization, waste reduction, and coordination performance.
- **Protective slack**: spare capacity, buffers, time margin, inventory, staff, and reserve options that preserve function under stress.
- **Resilience capacity**: the combined ability to absorb stress, reroute function, repair damage, and adapt.
- **Optimization fragility pressure**: the pressure created by tight coupling, single-point dependence, overload, deferred maintenance, and hidden risk transfer.
- **Slack-fragility gap**: the difference between resilience capacity and optimization fragility pressure.
- **Hidden risk transfer**: risk shifted to workers, households, small suppliers, communities, ecosystems, or future budgets.

## Interpretation

A system can be efficient in ordinary conditions while fragile under disruption. This model helps distinguish resilient efficiency from fragility-producing optimization.

The model is most useful for infrastructure planning, supply-chain review, public-sector capacity analysis, healthcare surge review, critical-systems governance, institutional design, and resilience investment strategy.
