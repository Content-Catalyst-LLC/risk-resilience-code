# Resilience, Justice, and Transformation Review Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing a resilience initiative, adaptation plan, recovery program, relocation process, transition strategy, ecological restoration project, public-service reform, or infrastructure investment.

## Distribution

- Who is protected first?
- Who receives benefits?
- Who bears costs?
- Whose losses are visible?
- Are vulnerable groups receiving protection proportionate to need?
- Are burdens distributed according to responsibility and capacity?

## Participation

- Were affected people involved before decisions were finalized?
- Is information accessible?
- Are language, disability, digital access, and time barriers addressed?
- Can communities influence design, implementation, and revision?
- Are local, Indigenous, professional, and lived forms of knowledge included respectfully?

## Recognition and Rights

- Are histories of harm acknowledged?
- Are communities treated as rights-holders rather than passive beneficiaries?
- Are land, culture, housing, health, water, food, safety, environmental, and information rights protected?
- Are appeal and remedy mechanisms available?

## Ethical Risk

- Could the initiative shift harm to less powerful groups?
- Could it cause displacement?
- Could it deepen inequality?
- Could it become coercive?
- Could it create long-term maladaptation?
- Are ecological harms included in the review?

## Accountability

- Are evidence, assumptions, and trade-offs documented?
- Are data disaggregated?
- Are outcomes auditable?
- Are complaints tracked?
- Are revisions made when harm appears?
- Is public reporting available?
