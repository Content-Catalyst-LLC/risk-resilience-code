# Resilience, Justice, and the Ethics of Transformation

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Resilience, Justice, and the Ethics of Transformation**

The workflows model how technical resilience, distributive justice, procedural justice, recognition, rights protection, institutional accountability, ecological governance, intergenerational responsibility, public legitimacy, data transparency, maladaptation risk, harm-shifting risk, exclusion risk, coercion risk, displacement risk, and unequal burdens shape justice-oriented transformation.

- Justice-oriented resilience measurement

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
resilience_justice_transformation_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `distributive_justice_index`: higher = fairer distribution of protection, benefits, burdens, recovery, and risk reduction
- `procedural_justice_index`: higher = stronger participation, access to information, and influence over decisions
- `harm_shifting_risk_index`: higher = greater risk of transferring harm to less powerful groups
- `coercion_risk_index`: higher = greater risk of forced or non-consensual transformation
