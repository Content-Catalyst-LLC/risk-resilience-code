# Path Dependence, Lock-In, and Resilience Traps

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Path Dependence, Lock-In, and Resilience Traps**

The workflows model how sunk cost, infrastructure rigidity, institutional inertia, incumbent power, social dependence, technological incompatibility, ecological feedback, alternative capacity, adaptive governance, public legitimacy, justice transition support, and reversibility shape lock-in risk, resilience trap pressure, and transformation readiness.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- Path dependence
- Lock-in
- Resilience traps
- Undesirable stability
- Social-ecological traps
- Carbon lock-in
- Infrastructure lock-in
- Institutional inertia
- Policy routines and governance path dependence
- Sunk costs and incumbent power
- Poverty-ecology feedbacks
- Just transition
- Windows of opportunity
- Transformative capacity
- Moving from stuck resilience to transformative resilience

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
path_dependence_lock_in_resilience_traps_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `sunk_cost_index`: higher = more investment committed to the incumbent pathway
- `incumbent_power_index`: higher = stronger political/economic defense of the pathway
- `alternative_capacity_index`: higher = more viable alternatives available
- `justice_transition_index`: higher = stronger protection for affected people during transition
