# Path Dependence, Lock-In, and Resilience Traps Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing locked-in infrastructure, maladaptive adaptation, carbon lock-in, social-ecological traps, institutional inertia, or just transition strategy.

## Diagnosing Lock-In

- What past decisions constrain current options?
- Which assets, laws, standards, routines, and expectations defend the incumbent pathway?
- What sunk costs make exit politically or financially difficult?
- Which institutions reproduce the pathway through routine procedures?
- Which actors benefit from continuity?

## Resilience Trap Conditions

- What undesirable state is being stabilized?
- Which social, ecological, economic, or political feedbacks reinforce that state?
- Are short-term coping strategies deepening long-term vulnerability?
- Are alternatives technically absent, politically blocked, financially weak, or socially inaccessible?

## Justice and Power

- Who benefits from the current pathway?
- Who bears the harm?
- Which communities are exposed, excluded, polluted, displaced, or under-resourced?
- Are workers and dependent communities protected during transition?
- Are Indigenous, local, marginalized, or affected communities given real voice?

## Transformation Capacity

- Are viable alternatives already being tested?
- Is there institutional flexibility to change course?
- Are coalitions prepared to support transformation?
- Are public legitimacy and trust strong enough for change?
- Are policies reducing dependence on the harmful pathway?

## Windows of Opportunity

- What crises, legal changes, funding cycles, political shifts, or technology changes could open space?
- Are alternatives ready before the window appears?
- Could emergency recovery reproduce the old trap?
- What would help the system move from stuck resilience to transformative resilience?
