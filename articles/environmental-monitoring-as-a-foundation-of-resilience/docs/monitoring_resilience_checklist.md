# Environmental Monitoring Resilience Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing environmental monitoring systems, early-warning systems, climate services, sensor networks, Earth-observation dashboards, public-health exposure platforms, or adaptive-governance workflows.

## Observation and Coverage

- Are key hazards, slow variables, ecosystems, infrastructures, and exposed communities monitored?
- Are under-protected places visible?
- Are data collected at useful spatial and temporal resolution?
- Are monitoring gaps documented?

## Data Quality and Architecture

- Are sensors calibrated and maintained?
- Are metadata, provenance, uncertainty, and version history documented?
- Are data schemas and dictionaries available?
- Can datasets be linked across agencies and domains?

## Warning and Action

- Are thresholds tied to clear institutional responsibilities?
- Do warnings reach people through trusted and accessible channels?
- Are actions triggered by monitoring signals?
- Are monitoring outcomes reviewed after events?

## Equity and Rights

- Are community observations included?
- Are Indigenous and local knowledge systems handled with consent and respect?
- Could data be misused for eviction, surveillance, extraction, or political manipulation?
- Are safeguards in place?

## Adaptive Governance

- Are monitoring results used to revise plans?
- Are failures audited?
- Are investments adjusted based on observed conditions?
- Are resilience claims testable?
