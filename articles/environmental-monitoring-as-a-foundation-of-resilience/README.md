# Environmental Monitoring as a Foundation of Resilience

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Environmental Monitoring as a Foundation of Resilience**

The workflows model how observation coverage, data quality, timeliness, interoperability, analytical capacity, warning dissemination, community validation, action linkage, rights safeguards, blind spots, uncertainty burden, decision lag, maintenance risk, and misuse risk shape monitoring-supported resilience.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- Environmental monitoring as resilience infrastructure
- Observation, interpretation, and action
- Early warning systems
- Slow variables and thresholds
- Hidden environmental stress
- Earth observation
- Ground-based sensing
- Sensor networks
- Situational awareness
- Environmental data architecture
- Data provenance and auditability
- Community monitoring
- Environmental justice
- Adaptive governance
- Monitoring-to-action pipelines
- Accountable environmental intelligence

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
environmental_monitoring_resilience_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `observation_coverage_index`: higher = broader monitoring coverage
- `data_quality_index`: higher = stronger validation, calibration, metadata, and reliability
- `blind_spot_index`: higher = larger monitoring gaps
- `decision_lag_index`: higher = longer delay between signal and action
