# Regenerative Resilience and the Repair of Living Systems

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Regenerative Resilience and the Repair of Living Systems**

The workflows model how ecosystem integrity, biodiversity, soil health, water function, habitat connectivity, local stewardship, governance accountability, justice and repair, monitoring quality, degradation pressure, fragmentation, extraction, pollution, climate stress, and maladaptation risk shape regenerative resilience and living-systems repair.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- Living-systems repair
- Ecosystem restoration
- Biodiversity as regenerative capacity
- Soil health
- Watershed function

- Restoration integrity
- Governance accountability
- Monitoring and measurement
- Maladaptation risk
- Systemic resilience under ecological degradation

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
regenerative_resilience_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `ecosystem_integrity_index`: higher = stronger ecosystem condition and function
- `soil_health_index`: higher = stronger soil regenerative capacity
- `water_function_index`: higher = stronger hydrological function and buffering
- `degradation_pressure_index`: higher = greater chronic ecological stress
- `maladaptation_risk_index`: higher = greater risk of harmful or superficial interventions
