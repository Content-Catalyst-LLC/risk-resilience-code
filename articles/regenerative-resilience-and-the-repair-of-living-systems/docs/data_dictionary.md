# Data Dictionary

| Column | Meaning |
|---|---|
| system_name | Name of the ecosystem, watershed, landscape, seascape, restoration area, urban ecological system, or regenerative project |
| jurisdiction | Geographic or institutional jurisdiction |
| system_type | System category such as watershed, forest, wetland, agricultural landscape, coastal system, urban ecosystem, grassland, river corridor, or restoration network |
| ecosystem_integrity_index | Ecological condition, structure, function, and continuity |
| biodiversity_index | Genetic, species, functional, and habitat diversity |
| soil_health_index | Soil organic matter, soil structure, biological activity, fertility, and water-holding capacity |
| water_function_index | Hydrology, infiltration, water quality, flood buffering, drought resilience, and flow regulation |
| connectivity_index | Habitat corridors, watershed connections, species movement, and landscape continuity |
| local_stewardship_index | Indigenous, local, community, farmer, fisher, practitioner, or participatory stewardship capacity |
| governance_accountability_index | Institutional capacity, monitoring, transparency, adaptive management, and accountability |
| justice_repair_index | Rights, participation, consent, equitable benefit, and repair of historical or distributional harm |
| monitoring_quality_index | Quality, continuity, transparency, and usefulness of ecological and social monitoring |
| degradation_pressure_index | Chronic ecological stress, land-use pressure, and cumulative system damage |
| fragmentation_pressure_index | Habitat fragmentation, isolation, and loss of ecological continuity |
| extraction_pressure_index | Pressure from overharvest, mining, logging, land conversion, water withdrawal, or unsustainable use |
| pollution_pressure_index | Chemical, nutrient, plastic, air, water, soil, and toxic contamination pressure |
| climate_stress_index | Heat, drought, flood, fire, sea-level rise, ocean warming, acidification, storm, or climate volatility stress |
| maladaptation_risk_index | Risk that resilience measures shift harm, simplify ecosystems, displace people, or deepen inequity |
