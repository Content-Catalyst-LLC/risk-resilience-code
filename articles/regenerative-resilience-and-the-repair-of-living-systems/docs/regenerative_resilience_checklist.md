# Regenerative Resilience Review Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing restoration projects, regenerative landscapes, nature-based resilience, watershed repair, food-system resilience, climate adaptation, or urban ecological infrastructure.

## Living-System Function

- Is ecosystem integrity improving?
- Are biodiversity, soil health, and water function measured?
- Are ecological processes being restored, not only visual greenness?
- Are connectivity and ecological memory protected?
- Are thresholds and regime-shift risks identified?

## Repair and Regeneration

- Does the intervention repair hydrology, soil, biodiversity, habitat, or food-web function?
- Are restoration outcomes monitored over time?
- Is the project capable of self-renewing ecological function?
- Are long-term maintenance and adaptive management funded?

## Justice and Stewardship

- Are Indigenous and local knowledge systems respected?
- Are land rights, tenure, consent, and community authority addressed?
- Are benefits distributed equitably?
- Are displacement, green gentrification, and conservation exclusion risks considered?
- Are frontline communities included in design and monitoring?

## Governance and Accountability

- Are project goals, methods, data, and uncertainties transparent?
- Are restoration claims auditable?
- Are outputs distinguished from outcomes?
- Are maladaptation risks reviewed?
- Are governance responsibilities clear after the initial project period?

## Climate and Systemic Risk

- Does the intervention reduce climate risk without increasing other risks?
- Does it support flood, drought, heat, fire, food, water, or health resilience?
- Does it repair root vulnerability rather than only protect assets?
