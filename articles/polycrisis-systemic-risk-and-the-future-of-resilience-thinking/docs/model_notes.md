# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual polycrisis, systemic-risk, and transformative-resilience scoring models.

The scores are not forecasts. They are diagnostic tools for making crisis-domain pressure, interaction coupling, systemic vulnerability, feedback amplification, threshold proximity, institutional capacity, legitimacy, regenerative capacity, equity, data auditability, and maladaptation risk visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Domain polycrisis pressure**: estimates pressure inside a specific domain such as climate, finance, cyber, food, health, infrastructure, ecology, migration, or governance.
- **Interaction pressure**: estimates how strongly stress in one crisis domain may amplify stress in another.
- **Transformative resilience**: estimates the capacity to adapt, learn, repair, and transform rather than merely recover.
- **Readiness gap**: compares transformative resilience against polycrisis pressure.
- **Regenerative capacity**: represents renewal of ecological, social, institutional, and material foundations of resilience.
- **Public legitimacy**: represents fairness, accountability, trust, competence, and willingness to cooperate.
- **Maladaptation risk**: represents the risk that resilience interventions shift harm, deepen vulnerability, or lock in fragile pathways.

## Interpretation

A jurisdiction may have moderate pressure in individual domains but high systemic risk when domains are strongly coupled.

The model is most useful for scenario planning, cross-domain stress testing, risk governance, public legitimacy review, ecological resilience analysis, fiscal resilience planning, and system-stewardship discussion.
