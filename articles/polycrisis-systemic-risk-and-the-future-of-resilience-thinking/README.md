# Polycrisis, Systemic Risk, and the Future of Resilience Thinking

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Polycrisis, Systemic Risk, and the Future of Resilience Thinking**

The workflows model how crisis-domain intensity, cross-domain coupling, systemic vulnerability, feedback amplification, threshold proximity, institutional capacity, public legitimacy, regenerative capacity, equity integration, data auditability, adaptive learning, and maladaptation risk shape polycrisis pressure and transformative resilience readiness.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- Polycrisis
- Systemic risk
- Future of resilience thinking
- Crisis-domain interaction
- Cascading and compound crises
- Cross-domain coupling
- Feedback amplification

- Planetary boundaries

- Maladaptation

- Transformative resilience

## Suggested Input Datasets

Expected domain file:

```text
polycrisis_domain_panel.csv
```

Expected interaction file:

```text
polycrisis_interaction_matrix.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `crisis_intensity_index`: higher = greater crisis pressure in that domain
- `institutional_capacity_index`: higher = stronger governance and response capacity
- `public_legitimacy_index`: higher = stronger trust, fairness, and accountability
- `maladaptation_risk_index`: higher = greater risk that interventions deepen fragility
