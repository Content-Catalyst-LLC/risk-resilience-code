# Tight Coupling and the Logic of Catastrophic Failure

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Tight Coupling and the Logic of Catastrophic Failure**

The workflows model how coupling strength, time compression, sequence rigidity, limited substitution, interactive complexity, hidden dependency, critical-node importance, buffering, modularity, redundancy, adaptive authority, and fallback capacity shape catastrophic-failure risk.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- Tight coupling
- Loose coupling
- Normal accident theory
- Interactive complexity
- Catastrophic-failure propagation
- Time compression
- Sequence rigidity
- Limited substitution
- Critical-node dependence
- Infrastructure interdependence
- Digital automation and high-speed dependence
- Supply chains, healthcare, energy, and water
- Limits of control, procedure, and compliance
- Justice and unequal exposure to coupling risk
- Resilience room, fallback capacity, and system design

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
tight_coupling_catastrophic_failure_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `coupling_strength_index`: higher = stronger dependence between system parts
- `time_compression_index`: higher = less time between disturbance and consequence
- `buffering_index`: higher = stronger protective buffers
- `adaptive_authority_index`: higher = stronger ability to pause, reroute, or respond locally
