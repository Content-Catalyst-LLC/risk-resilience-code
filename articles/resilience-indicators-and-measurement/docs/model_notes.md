# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual resilience-indicator scoring models.

The scores are not objective measurements of resilience. They are diagnostic tools for making measurement assumptions visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Capacity-asset-process score**: estimates resilience support conditions through capacity, resources, and governance processes.
- **Performance-outcome score**: estimates observed or expected resilience performance through outcomes, service continuity, recovery, adaptive learning, institutional capacity, ecological condition, and social protection.
- **Measurement vulnerability**: estimates exposure to misleading measurement through vulnerability, exposure, distributional inequality, uncertainty, data gaps, and false precision risk.
- **Measured resilience**: combines indicator-family scores, equity protection, financial protection, and lower measurement vulnerability.
- **Equity-adjusted resilience**: reduces measured resilience when distributional inequality is high or equity protection is weak.
- **Indicator confidence**: estimates the reliability of the measurement framework based on uncertainty, data quality, and false precision risk.

## Interpretation

A high resilience score can be misleading if data quality is weak, uncertainty is high, or distributional inequality is hidden.

The model is most useful for comparative diagnosis, indicator design, dashboard review, resilience scorecard critique, and measurement-quality assessment.
