# Risk, Poverty, and Development Fragility

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Risk, Poverty, and Development Fragility**

The workflows model how risk exposure, multidimensional poverty, institutional weakness, livelihood precarity, service deficits, conflict pressure, climate stress, displacement pressure, social protection, household buffers, adaptive capacity, service continuity, institutional trust, and community voice shape development fragility.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- Poverty as a resilience problem
- Development fragility
- Multidimensional deprivation
- Risk exposure
- Shock amplification
- Development scarring
- Fragility as exposure plus insufficient resilience
- Basic service breakdown
- Livelihood precarity
- Conflict and displacement
- Climate, food, energy, and water stress
- Social protection
- Institutional trust and public legitimacy
- Resilient development

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
risk_poverty_development_fragility_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `risk_exposure_index`: higher = greater exposure to shocks
- `multidimensional_poverty_index`: higher = deeper overlapping deprivation
- `social_protection_index`: higher = stronger social protection coverage
- `institutional_trust_index`: higher = stronger public legitimacy and trust
