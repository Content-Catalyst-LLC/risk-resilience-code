# Risk, Poverty, and Development Fragility Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing poverty-reduction strategy, fragile-context programming, climate adaptation, service delivery, development finance, social protection, or resilience governance.

## Poverty and Deprivation

- Is poverty measured beyond income?
- Are health, education, housing, sanitation, energy, food security, mobility, and safety included?
- Are repeated small shocks tracked, not only headline crises?
- Are households near irreversible loss identified?

## Risk Exposure

- Are climate, conflict, price, disease, displacement, and service-disruption risks assessed together?
- Are risks mapped by geography, livelihood, gender, age, disability, migration status, and tenure where relevant?
- Are compounding shocks and cascading effects considered?

## Buffers and Resilience Sufficiency

- Do households have savings, secure housing, food reserves, care networks, and access to social protection?
- Can basic services continue during stress?
- Are livelihoods diversified and protected?
- Are adaptive capacities being built before crisis?

## Institutions and Trust

- Are public institutions trusted by affected communities?
- Are services delivered fairly?
- Are community voices included in decisions?
- Are data, finance, and aid systems accountable?

## Development Scarring

- Are schooling, health, debt, asset loss, displacement, and trauma tracked after shocks?
- Do recovery programs prevent long-term scarring?
- Are interventions preventing future vulnerability rather than only repairing past harm?
