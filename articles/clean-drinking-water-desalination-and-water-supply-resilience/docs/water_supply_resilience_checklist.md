# Clean Drinking Water and Water-Supply Resilience Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing drinking-water systems, desalination projects, reuse systems, small-system resilience, water safety plans, or water justice strategy.

## Source-to-Tap Safety

- Are source waters protected from contamination?
- Are treatment processes matched to known and emerging hazards?
- Are distribution-system risks monitored?
- Are household-level exposure risks included?
- Are water safety plans current and actively used?

## Desalination and Reuse

- Is desalination part of an integrated water portfolio rather than a standalone fix?
- Are energy demand and renewable integration assessed?
- Is brine managed with environmental safeguards?
- Are marine intake and discharge impacts monitored?
- Are reuse systems governed with strong treatment, testing, and public communication?

## Infrastructure Resilience

- Are leaks, pressure loss, aging pipes, service lines, tanks, pumps, and treatment facilities tracked?
- Is backup power available for critical water operations?
- Are spare parts, chemicals, operators, cyber fallback, and emergency protocols available?
- Can the system maintain safe service during power outages or supply-chain disruptions?

## Water Quality and Monitoring

- Are microbial, chemical, salinity, metals, nutrient, and emerging contaminant risks assessed?
- Are sampling, sensors, laboratories, and public dashboards reliable?
- Are boil-water notices and do-not-drink orders communicated clearly?
- Are monitoring results tied to corrective action?

## Justice and Affordability

- Are low-income households protected from unaffordable bills and shutoffs?
- Are rural, tribal, island, informal, and small-system communities included?
- Are contaminated communities prioritized for remediation?
- Are affected residents included in decisions?
