# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual drinking-water resilience, desalination sustainability, and source-to-tap safety models.

The scores are not objective certifications of drinking-water safety. They are diagnostic tools for making source protection, treatment capacity, distribution reliability, monitoring quality, supply diversity, energy resilience, affordability, governance capacity, contamination risk, infrastructure aging, salinity pressure, energy dependence, brine burden, and access inequality visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Water resilience capacity**: estimates the strength of the source-to-tap system.
- **Water-system risk pressure**: estimates pressure from contamination, aging infrastructure, salinity, energy dependence, brine, and unequal access.
- **Drinking-water resilience**: estimates whether the system can provide safe, reliable, affordable water under stress.
- **Source-to-tap resilience gap**: compares water-system capacity with risk pressure.
- **Desalination sustainability**: assesses energy, brine, marine impact, affordability, renewable integration, and governance transparency.
- **Water safety planning**: uses proactive risk management from catchment to consumer.

## Interpretation

A water system can have physical water availability while still being fragile if treatment, distribution, monitoring, affordability, governance, energy resilience, or access equity are weak.

The model is most useful for utility planning, drinking-water safety planning, desalination review, infrastructure renewal, small-system support, water justice analysis, and resilient water-supply strategy.
