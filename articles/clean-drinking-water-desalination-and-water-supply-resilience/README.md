# Clean Drinking Water, Desalination, and Water-Supply Resilience

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Clean Drinking Water, Desalination, and Water-Supply Resilience**

The workflows model how source protection, treatment capacity, distribution reliability, monitoring quality, supply diversity, energy resilience, affordability, governance capacity, contamination risk, infrastructure aging, salinity pressure, energy dependence, brine burden, and access inequality shape drinking-water resilience.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- Clean drinking water as a public-health foundation
- Source-to-tap resilience
- Source-water protection
- Drinking-water quality
- Treatment, filtration, and disinfection
- Desalination as strategic supply
- Reverse osmosis and thermal desalination
- Brine management
- Energy demand and renewable integration
- Wastewater reuse and circular water systems
- Distribution-network fragility
- Water safety plans
- Monitoring and testing
- Affordability and water justice
- Water-energy-cyber interdependence
- Governance, finance, and public trust

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
drinking_water_resilience_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `source_protection_index`: higher = stronger watershed, aquifer, reservoir, or recharge-zone protection
- `treatment_capacity_index`: higher = stronger ability to treat known risks
- `contamination_risk_index`: higher = greater untreated or residual risk
- `access_inequality_index`: higher = more unequal access to safe and affordable water
