# The Future of Resilience Thinking

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**The Future of Resilience Thinking**

The workflows model how systemic-risk capacity, governance integration, justice-oriented transformation, regenerative capacity, local capability, technological accountability, planetary alignment, investment readiness, data accountability, learning capacity, fragmentation risk, maladaptation risk, inequality risk, ecological overshoot, technological dependency, and conceptual vagueness shape future-oriented resilience readiness.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- The future of resilience thinking
- Systemic risk
- Whole-system governance
- Justice-oriented transformation
- Regenerative capacity
- Local resilience and multilevel governance
- AI and technological dependency
- Cyber-physical resilience
- Planetary boundaries
- Biosphere integrity
- Resilience investment
- Measurement and accountability
- Data provenance and evidence trails
- Conceptual discipline
- Maladaptation and harm-shifting
- Transformation under uncertainty

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
future_resilience_framework_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `systemic_risk_capacity_index`: higher = stronger capacity to analyze cascading and cross-sector risk
- `justice_transformation_index`: higher = stronger justice-oriented transformation
- `maladaptation_risk_index`: higher = greater risk of shifting harm or locking in fragility
- `conceptual_vagueness_index`: higher = weaker conceptual clarity around what resilience means
