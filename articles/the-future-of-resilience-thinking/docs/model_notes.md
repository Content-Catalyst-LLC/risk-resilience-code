# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual future-oriented resilience scoring models.

The scores are not forecasts or objective certifications. They are diagnostic tools for making the next generation of resilience thinking visible across systemic risk, governance, justice, regeneration, local capability, technology, planetary limits, investment, data accountability, and learning.

## Core Concepts

- **Future resilience capacity**: estimates whether a strategy is prepared for systemic risk, transformation, technological dependency, planetary limits, and justice demands.
- **Future fragility pressure**: estimates risk from fragmentation, maladaptation, inequality, ecological overshoot, technological dependency, and conceptual vagueness.
- **Conceptual discipline**: estimates whether the resilience strategy is clear, evidence-based, accountable, and specific rather than vague.
- **Future resilience readiness gap**: compares future resilience capacity against fragility pressure.
- **Systemic-risk capacity**: ability to understand cascades, dependencies, interactions, thresholds, and cross-sector risk.
- **Governance integration**: ability to coordinate across sectors, levels of government, institutions, and time horizons.
- **Regenerative capacity**: ability to repair ecological, social, institutional, and material foundations of resilience.
- **Technological accountability**: governance of AI, cyber risk, digital dependency, data provenance, auditability, and fallback capacity.
- **Planetary alignment**: compatibility with biosphere stability, planetary boundaries, and ecological limits.

## Interpretation

A resilience strategy may be strong at emergency response while weak on transformation, justice, technological accountability, or planetary limits.

The model is most useful for reviewing climate adaptation strategies, infrastructure resilience plans, public-sector resilience frameworks, local risk governance, AI and cyber resilience, ecological restoration strategies, and long-term resilience investment plans.
