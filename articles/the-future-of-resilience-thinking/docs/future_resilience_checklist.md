# Future Resilience Thinking Review Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing a resilience strategy, adaptation plan, infrastructure framework, AI governance program, public-sector reform, ecological restoration strategy, disaster-risk framework, or long-term resilience investment plan.

## Systemic Risk

- Are cascading and compound risks included?
- Are cross-sector dependencies mapped?
- Are common-mode failures identified?
- Are thresholds and slow-moving stresses included?
- Are shock libraries or scenario matrices used?

## Governance

- Are roles and responsibilities clear?
- Is coordination built across sectors and jurisdictions?
- Are local governments resourced?
- Are communities meaningfully involved?
- Is adaptive learning built into the strategy?

## Justice and Transformation

- Who is protected first?
- Who bears burdens?
- Are affected communities involved before decisions are finalized?
- Are rights, recognition, participation, and accountability included?
- Does the strategy repair vulnerability or merely protect the current system?

## Regeneration and Planetary Limits

- Does the strategy repair ecological foundations?
- Are soil, water, biodiversity, and ecosystem function considered?
- Is the strategy compatible with planetary limits?
- Does it reduce ecological overshoot or displace it elsewhere?

## Technology and Evidence

- Are AI, digital systems, cyber risk, and automation dependency governed?
- Are data provenance and audit trails documented?
- Is fallback capacity preserved?
- Are resilience claims testable and contestable?
- Are uncertainty and data gaps disclosed?

## Investment

- Is prevention funded?
- Is maintenance funded?
- Are local institutions resourced?
- Are social protection and ecological restoration treated as resilience investments?
- Are public goods valued alongside financial returns?
