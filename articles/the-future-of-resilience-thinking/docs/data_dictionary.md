# Data Dictionary

| Column | Meaning |
|---|---|
| strategy_name | Name of the resilience strategy, framework, plan, investment program, or governance approach |
| jurisdiction | Geographic or institutional jurisdiction |
| strategy_domain | Domain such as climate adaptation, infrastructure, public institutions, AI governance, ecological restoration, food systems, finance, local risk governance, or urban resilience |
| systemic_risk_capacity_index | Capacity to analyze cascading, compound, transboundary, and cross-sector systemic risk |
| governance_integration_index | Strength of coordination across sectors, institutions, jurisdictions, and time horizons |
| justice_transformation_index | Strength of justice-oriented transformation, fair protection, participation, recognition, rights, and accountability |
| regenerative_capacity_index | Capacity to repair ecological, social, institutional, and material foundations of resilience |
| local_capability_index | Local governance capacity, community knowledge, public trust, and place-based implementation ability |
| technological_accountability_index | AI governance, cyber resilience, data provenance, auditability, model oversight, and fallback capacity |
| planetary_alignment_index | Compatibility with biosphere stability, ecological limits, and planetary-boundary constraints |
| investment_readiness_index | Financing, maintenance, prevention, adaptation, and long-term public investment readiness |
| data_accountability_index | Evidence quality, data provenance, transparency, disaggregation, auditability, and contestability |
| learning_capacity_index | Adaptive management, institutional learning, review, revision, and feedback loops |
| fragmentation_risk_index | Risk from silos, weak coordination, disconnected planning, and institutional fragmentation |
| maladaptation_risk_index | Risk that resilience actions shift harm, lock in fragility, or deepen vulnerability |
| inequality_risk_index | Risk that resilience benefits and burdens are distributed unequally |
| ecological_overshoot_risk_index | Risk that strategies depend on or worsen ecological overshoot and biosphere destabilization |
| technological_dependency_risk_index | Risk from brittle digital dependency, AI systems, cyber exposure, cloud concentration, or automation reliance |
| conceptual_vagueness_index | Risk that resilience is used as a vague label without clear system boundaries, functions, evidence, or accountability |
