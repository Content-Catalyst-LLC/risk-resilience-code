# Public Institutional Resilience Strategy Review Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing public-sector resilience, continuity planning, institutional stress testing, procurement resilience, local-government readiness, public-service continuity, or administrative reform.

## Foresight and Preparedness

- Does the institution use horizon scanning and strategic foresight?
- Are scenarios connected to budgets, procurement, staffing, and operations?
- Are early-warning triggers defined?
- Are stress tests or tabletop exercises conducted?

## Continuity of Core Functions

- Which public functions must continue during disruption?
- Are critical staff, systems, data, vendors, and legal authorities identified?
- Are manual fallback procedures available?
- Are vulnerable service users considered?

## Administrative Capacity

- Are staffing levels adequate under surge conditions?
- Is institutional memory documented?
- Are emergency delegations clear?
- Are local implementation partners resourced?

## Finance and Procurement

- Is prevention funded?
- Are maintenance and preparedness protected from cuts?
- Are emergency procurement rules documented?
- Are critical suppliers mapped?
- Are procurement risks monitored?

## Digital Resilience

- Are digital systems secure and backed up?
- Are offline or in-person service channels preserved?
- Are automated decisions auditable and contestable?
- Are accessibility and language needs addressed?

## Legitimacy, Justice, and Accountability

- Is public communication timely, clear, and honest?
- Are services accessible to marginalized groups?
- Are appeal and remedy mechanisms available?
- Are outcomes disaggregated?
- Are after-action reviews public where appropriate?

## Learning

- Do exercises and crises lead to changed practice?
- Are budgets, standards, procurement, training, and continuity plans updated?
- Is community feedback included in learning?
