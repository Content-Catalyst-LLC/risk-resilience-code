# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual risk-governance and adaptive-institution scoring models.

The scores are not objective measurements of institutional quality. They are diagnostic tools for making assumptions visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Risk governance quality**: estimates the capacity to anticipate, appraise, coordinate, participate, remain transparent, maintain legitimacy, learn, preserve institutional memory, and incorporate justice.
- **Adaptive institutional capacity**: estimates the capacity to monitor feedback, revise policy, coordinate across sectors, learn, remember, participate, maintain legitimacy, and respond to unequal vulnerability.
- **Governance vulnerability**: estimates exposure created by fragmentation, uncertainty load, capture risk, systemic risk exposure, vulnerability exposure, weak coordination, weak transparency, and weak learning.
- **Resilience governance score**: combines governance quality, adaptive institutional capacity, lower vulnerability, and justice orientation.

## Interpretation

An institution can have formal authority and expert knowledge while still lacking adaptive governance capacity if coordination, participation, legitimacy, and learning are weak.

The model is most useful for comparative diagnosis, governance review, resilience planning, and scenario discussion.
