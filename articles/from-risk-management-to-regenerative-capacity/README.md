# From Risk Management to Regenerative Capacity

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**From Risk Management to Regenerative Capacity**

The workflows model how risk-management capacity, ecological restoration, social capacity, institutional learning, justice orientation, long-term investment, livelihood viability, regenerative finance, depletion pressure, maladaptation risk, extractive pressure, institutional fatigue, and chronic vulnerability shape regenerative resilience.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- Defensive risk management
- Regenerative resilience
- Ecological restoration
- Ecological function
- Soil, water, biodiversity, and landscape renewal
- Social capacity
- Institutional learning
- Institutional memory
- Public trust
- Justice orientation
- Community participation
- Livelihood viability
- Long-term investment
- Regenerative finance
- Depletion pressure
- Maladaptation risk
- Extractive pressure
- Institutional fatigue
- Chronic vulnerability
- Recovery-only bias

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
regenerative_resilience_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `risk_management_capacity_index`: higher = stronger protective risk management
- `ecological_restoration_index`: higher = stronger ecological renewal
- `depletion_pressure_index`: higher = greater ecological, social, or institutional depletion
- `maladaptation_risk_index`: higher = greater risk that short-term protection increases long-term vulnerability
