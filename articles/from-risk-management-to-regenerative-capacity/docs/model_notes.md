# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual regenerative resilience scoring models.

The scores are not objective measurements of ecological, social, or institutional regeneration. They are diagnostic tools for making renewal assumptions visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Defensive risk management**: estimates hazard assessment, exposure reduction, preparedness, adaptive governance, institutional learning, public trust, participation, and long-term investment.
- **Regenerative capacity**: estimates ecological restoration, ecological function, social capacity, institutional learning, justice orientation, livelihood viability, long-term investment, and regenerative finance.
- **Depletion and maladaptation pressure**: estimates ecological, social, and institutional depletion; maladaptation; extractive pressure; institutional fatigue; chronic vulnerability; recovery-only bias; weak ecological function; and weak justice orientation.
- **Regenerative resilience**: combines regenerative capacity, defensive risk management, lower depletion pressure, justice orientation, and ecological function.
- **Renewal balance**: compares regeneration with depletion pressure.

## Interpretation

A system can be well protected against acute risk while still carrying a negative renewal balance if it is degrading ecological, social, or institutional foundations.

The model is most useful for comparative diagnosis, regeneration planning, resilience investment review, and systems analysis.
