# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual public-institution stress-test scoring models.

The scores are not objective measurements of institutional resilience. They are diagnostic tools for making stress-readiness assumptions visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Stress readiness**: estimates whether a public institution has essential-function clarity, capacity margins, visible dependencies, resilient staff, digital resilience, legal clarity, coordination, equity protection, public trust, recovery capacity, learning capacity, and accountability mechanisms.
- **Stress vulnerability**: estimates exposure created by overload pressure, institutional fragmentation, hidden fragility, vendor dependency, compound stress, thin capacity margins, weak dependency visibility, weak digital resilience, weak workforce resilience, and low trust.
- **Institutional recovery**: estimates the ability to restore function through recovery capacity, backup systems, mutual aid, fiscal reserves, learning, accountability, and coordination.
- **Resilience-adjusted stress risk**: combines vulnerability, low readiness, weak recovery, compound stress, and equity gaps.

## Interpretation

A public institution can appear functional under normal conditions while carrying high stress vulnerability.

The model is most useful for comparative diagnosis, preparedness planning, continuity review, public accountability analysis, and institutional reform.
