# Stress Testing Public Institutions

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Stress Testing Public Institutions**

The workflows model how essential-function clarity, capacity margins, dependency visibility, workforce resilience, digital resilience, legal authority, coordination, equity protection, public trust, overload pressure, fragmentation, hidden fragility, vendor dependency, compound stress, and recovery capacity shape public-institution stress readiness.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- Public-institution stress readiness
- Essential-function clarity
- Capacity margins and surge capacity
- Dependency visibility
- Workforce resilience
- Digital and cyber resilience
- Legal authority clarity
- Coordination capacity
- Equity protection
- Public trust
- Recovery capacity
- Backup systems
- Mutual aid
- Fiscal reserves
- Learning capacity
- Accountability mechanisms
- Overload pressure
- Institutional fragmentation
- Hidden fragility
- Vendor dependency
- Compound stress exposure

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
public_institution_stress_test_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `essential_function_clarity_index`: higher = clearer essential-function prioritization
- `capacity_margin_index`: higher = stronger slack and surge capacity
- `overload_pressure_index`: higher = greater overload pressure
- `hidden_fragility_index`: higher = greater hidden fragility
