# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual early warning and preparedness scoring models.

The scores are not objective measurements of disaster readiness. They are diagnostic tools for making warning-chain and preparedness assumptions visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Warning effectiveness**: estimates the capacity of a system to convert risk knowledge, forecast skill, lead time, communication, trust, preparedness, and response capacity into usable warning.
- **Preparedness system score**: estimates household, community, institutional, protocol, response, and equity readiness.
- **Warning vulnerability**: estimates exposure created by vulnerable populations, access barriers, fragmentation, uncertainty, missed alarms, false alarms, weak trust, weak communication, and weak response capacity.
- **Early action readiness**: combines warning effectiveness, preparedness capacity, lower vulnerability, and equity access.

## Interpretation

A warning system can have strong forecasting capacity and still fail if communication, trust, preparedness, response capacity, and access are weak.

The model is most useful for comparative diagnosis, preparedness planning, warning-system review, and scenario discussion.
