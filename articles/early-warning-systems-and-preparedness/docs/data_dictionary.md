# Data Dictionary

| Column | Meaning |
|---|---|
| system_name | Name of the early warning or preparedness system |
| jurisdiction | Geographic or institutional jurisdiction |
| hazard_type | Hazard type such as flood, heat, cyclone, drought, wildfire, landslide, epidemic, or multi-hazard |
| risk_knowledge_index | Knowledge of hazards, exposure, vulnerability, and likely impacts |
| forecast_skill_index | Reliability, calibration, accuracy, and decision relevance of monitoring and forecasting |
| lead_time_index | Amount of usable time available before impact |
| communication_reach_index | Degree to which warnings reach affected people through accessible channels |
| trust_strength_index | Credibility of messengers, institutions, and warning sources |
| preparedness_capacity_index | General preparedness capacity before warnings are issued |
| response_capacity_index | Operational ability to act once warning is issued |
| protocol_clarity_index | Clarity of thresholds, responsibilities, action menus, and authority |
| equity_access_index | Degree to which vulnerable communities can receive, understand, and act on warnings |
| household_preparedness_index | Household readiness, supplies, plans, and ability to respond |
| community_preparedness_index | Community networks, mutual aid, local organizations, and local leadership |
| institutional_preparedness_index | Agency plans, staffing, coordination, training, and logistics |
| uncertainty_burden_index | Degree of uncertainty, ambiguity, and incomplete information |
| access_barrier_index | Transport, language, disability, financial, digital, shelter, or trust barriers |
| false_alarm_strain_index | Burden or credibility strain from precautionary warnings that do not materialize |
| missed_alarm_risk_index | Risk that warning thresholds are too late, weak, or unclear |
| institutional_fragmentation_index | Degree of disconnected responsibility or weak interagency coordination |
| vulnerability_exposure_index | Exposure of vulnerable populations, systems, or places to harm |
