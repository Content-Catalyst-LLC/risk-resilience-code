# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual scenario-matrix and shock-library resilience planning models.

The scores are not objective measurements of the future. They are diagnostic tools for making scenario assumptions, shock-library quality, planning readiness, and blind spots visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Scenario matrix quality**: estimates whether plausible futures are connected to shocks, essential functions, dependencies, triggers, thresholds, equity, and stress tests.
- **Shock-library reliability**: estimates whether the shock catalog is specific, operationally useful, updated, community-validated, and mapped to essential functions and dependencies.
- **Planning actionability**: estimates whether scenario and shock analysis connects to ownership, triggers, thresholds, stress tests, adaptive decisions, and update cycles.
- **Blind-spot pressure**: estimates risk from compound exposure, missing shocks, stale assumptions, scenario theater, untested fragility, and weak community validation.
- **Resilience-planning readiness**: combines scenario matrix quality, shock-library reliability, actionability, adaptive decision capacity, and lower blind-spot pressure.

## Interpretation

A planning system can have polished scenarios and still be weak if it lacks shock specificity, trigger readiness, ownership, action linkage, community validation, or update cycles.

The model is most useful for scenario planning review, shock-library audits, resilience planning diagnostics, preparedness design, and stress-test governance.
