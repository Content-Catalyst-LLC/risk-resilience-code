# Data Dictionary

| Column | Meaning |
|---|---|
| institution_or_system | Name of the institution, governance system, or resilience-governance arrangement |
| jurisdiction | Geographic or institutional jurisdiction |
| risk_domain | Risk domain such as climate, public health, finance, cyber, infrastructure, disaster risk, or ecological risk |
| adaptive_capacity_index | Flexibility, feedback use, experimentation, and policy revision capacity |
| accountability_capacity_index | Answerability, review, oversight, remedy, and correction capacity |
| public_legitimacy_index | Trust, fairness, competence, inclusion, and public acceptance |
| transparency_index | Openness, documentation, uncertainty communication, and public explanation |
| participation_strength_index | Meaningful public, stakeholder, and community influence |
| coordination_capacity_index | Cross-sector and cross-scale institutional alignment |
| learning_capacity_index | Capacity to learn from feedback, crisis reviews, monitoring, and failure |
| institutional_memory_index | Ability to preserve lessons beyond political and budget cycles |
| justice_orientation_index | Attention to vulnerability, unequal exposure, and fair distribution |
| oversight_strength_index | Strength of administrative, legal, political, professional, or community oversight |
| remedy_availability_index | Availability of appeal, repair, compensation, or corrective remedy |
| correction_capacity_index | Ability to change policy, operations, or resource allocation after failure |
| fragmentation_index | Degree of siloed authority, coordination gaps, or responsibility diffusion |
| capture_risk_index | Risk that powerful interests distort governance processes |
| trust_erosion_index | Degree of declining public confidence caused by failure, opacity, unfairness, or exclusion |
| vulnerability_exposure_index | Exposure of vulnerable communities, systems, or populations to harm |
| systemic_risk_exposure_index | Exposure to cascading, cross-sector, or transboundary risk |
