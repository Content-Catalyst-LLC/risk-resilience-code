# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual resilience-governance, accountability, and legitimacy scoring models.

The scores are not objective measurements of public legitimacy or institutional quality. They are diagnostic tools for making governance assumptions visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Resilience governance quality**: estimates the capacity to adapt, remain accountable, maintain legitimacy, communicate transparently, coordinate, learn, preserve memory, and incorporate justice.
- **Accountability-legitimacy capacity**: estimates the strength of accountability, legitimacy, transparency, oversight, remedy, correction, participation, and justice orientation.
- **Governance vulnerability**: estimates exposure created by fragmentation, capture risk, trust erosion, vulnerable exposure, systemic risk exposure, weak accountability, weak transparency, and weak learning.
- **Legitimate resilience governance score**: combines governance quality, accountability-legitimacy capacity, lower vulnerability, and justice orientation.

## Interpretation

A governance system can be flexible and innovative while still lacking legitimacy if it is not transparent, accountable, reviewable, just, or trusted.

The model is most useful for comparative diagnosis, governance review, public accountability analysis, and resilience planning.
