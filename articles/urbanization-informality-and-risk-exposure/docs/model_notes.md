# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual urban informality, risk exposure, and inclusive protection scoring models.

The scores are not objective certifications of settlement safety. They are diagnostic tools for making hazard exposure, urban vulnerability, infrastructure deficits, tenure insecurity, livelihood precarity, community adaptation, institutional protection, and protection gaps visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Hazard exposure**: flood, heat, landslide, fire, and climate-related exposure.
- **Urban vulnerability**: housing vulnerability, infrastructure deficits, livelihood precarity, tenure insecurity, displacement pressure, and weak social protection.
- **Risk exposure**: combined hazard exposure, vulnerability, climate stress, infrastructure deficits, and displacement pressure.
- **Inclusive protection capacity**: service access, tenure security, community adaptation, institutional protection, social protection, data visibility, and reduced infrastructure deficits.
- **Urban protection gap**: inclusive protection capacity minus risk exposure.

## Interpretation

A settlement can have high community adaptation capacity while still facing severe risk if formal service access, infrastructure, tenure security, and institutional protection remain weak.
