# Inclusive Urban Resilience Checklist

## Risk and Exposure

- Are flood, heat, fire, landslide, disease, and infrastructure risks mapped together?
- Are under-serviced and informal settlements visible in official risk data?
- Are repeated small shocks tracked, not only major disasters?
- Are climate projections connected to land and infrastructure decisions?

## Land, Housing, and Tenure

- Are residents protected from eviction during upgrading or adaptation?
- Are tenure-security pathways included?
- Are renters, informal tenants, and undocumented residents considered?
- Are relocation plans rights-based, participatory, livelihood-sensitive, and adequately funded?

## Services and Infrastructure

- Are water, sanitation, drainage, roads, electricity, waste, health, and emergency access included?
- Are infrastructure investments prioritized by vulnerability, not only asset value?
- Are service reliability, affordability, and accessibility measured?
- Are maintenance responsibilities and budgets defined?

## Livelihoods and Social Protection

- Are informal workers included in resilience planning?
- Are income losses, asset losses, and recovery needs considered?
- Are social protection and recovery mechanisms accessible?

## Community-Led Adaptation

- Are community organizations involved from the start?
- Are women, renters, migrants, disabled residents, youth, older people, and informal workers included?
- Are community maps and local knowledge used responsibly?

## Data and Accountability

- Who owns and controls settlement data?
- Could mapping increase eviction or surveillance risk?
- Are protection gaps publicly visible?
- Are investments tracked by settlement and vulnerability?
