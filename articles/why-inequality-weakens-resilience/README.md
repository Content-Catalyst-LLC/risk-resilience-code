# Why Inequality Weakens Resilience

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Why Inequality Weakens Resilience**

The workflows model how system capacity, distributed protection, household buffers, service access, institutional trust, adaptive capacity, social protection, community voice, exposure concentration, multidimensional deprivation, social exclusion, recovery inequality, digital exclusion, and fiscal stress shape equality-adjusted resilience.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- Inequality as a structural driver of fragility
- Distributed capacity
- Unequal exposure
- Household buffers
- Adaptive capacity
- Service access
- Infrastructure inequality
- Territorial inequality
- Poverty and fragility
- Climate risk and social vulnerability
- Health, education, and human capability
- Digital, financial, and administrative exclusion
- Institutional trust
- Public legitimacy
- Recovery inequality
- Pre-distribution of resilience
- Equality-adjusted resilience

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
inequality_resilience_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `service_access_index`: higher = stronger practical access to public services
- `distributed_protection_index`: higher = protection reaches vulnerable groups more evenly
- `exposure_concentration_index`: higher = hazards are more concentrated among disadvantaged groups
- `multidimensional_deprivation_index`: higher = overlapping deprivation is more severe
