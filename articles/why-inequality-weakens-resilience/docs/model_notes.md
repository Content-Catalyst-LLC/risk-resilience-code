# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual inequality-adjusted resilience scoring models.

The scores are not objective certifications of social resilience. They are diagnostic tools for making aggregate capacity, distributed protection, household buffers, service access, institutional trust, adaptive capacity, inequality pressure, exposure concentration, deprivation, exclusion, recovery inequality, and resilience gaps visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Aggregate resilience capacity**: estimates broad system capacity from institutions, services, adaptation, social protection, trust, household buffers, and community voice.
- **Inequality pressure**: estimates stress from exposure concentration, multidimensional deprivation, social exclusion, recovery inequality, digital exclusion, and fiscal stress.
- **Equality-adjusted resilience**: estimates whether resilience capacity is distributed rather than concentrated.
- **Resilience inequality gap**: compares aggregate resilience with equality-adjusted resilience.
- **Protection gap**: compares distributed protection against exposure concentration.
- **Pre-distribution of resilience**: refers to housing, services, income, care, legal recognition, and public protection being in place before crisis occurs.

## Interpretation

A place can have high aggregate capacity but weak equality-adjusted resilience if protection is concentrated and disadvantaged people or territories absorb risk without adequate support.

The model is most useful for vulnerability assessment, social-protection planning, infrastructure targeting, climate adaptation, regional development, public legitimacy analysis, and resilience governance review.
