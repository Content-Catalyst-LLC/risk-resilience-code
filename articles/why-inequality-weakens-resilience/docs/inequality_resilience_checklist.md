# Inequality and Resilience Review Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing vulnerability, social protection, climate adaptation, infrastructure investment, regional development, disaster recovery, public legitimacy, or resilience governance.

## Exposure and Protection

- Are hazards concentrated among lower-income or marginalized groups?
- Are under-served places receiving proportional protection?
- Are protection gaps visible by neighborhood, region, income, race, gender, disability, age, migration status, and tenure where appropriate?
- Are asset values being allowed to dominate investment decisions?

## Buffers and Adaptive Capacity

- Do households have savings, secure housing, food access, healthcare, care support, and transportation?
- Are social protection systems accessible before and after shocks?
- Are informal workers, renters, undocumented people, disabled people, and digitally excluded people included?
- Are repeated small shocks tracked, not only headline disasters?

## Services and Infrastructure

- Are water, sanitation, electricity, drainage, transport, health, schools, communications, and emergency access reliable and affordable?
- Are service quality and reliability measured, not only nominal coverage?
- Are maintenance gaps and infrastructure backlogs visible?

## Trust, Voice, and Legitimacy

- Are affected communities able to shape risk priorities?
- Are institutions trusted by marginalized communities?
- Are data, models, and decisions transparent?
- Are appeals and remedies accessible?

## Pre-Distribution

- What protections must be in place before the next shock?
- Is prevention being funded before emergency response?
- Are policies reducing the need for rescue by reducing structural vulnerability?
