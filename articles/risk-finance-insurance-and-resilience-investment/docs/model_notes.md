# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual risk finance, insurance, and resilience investment scoring models.

The scores are not objective measurements of financial resilience. They are diagnostic tools for making risk-finance assumptions visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Resilience finance capacity**: estimates the strength of risk visibility, insurance coverage, pre-arranged finance, fiscal capacity, resilience investment, mitigation incentives, equity protection, affordability, public risk pools, contingent credit, catastrophe bonds, and social protection.
- **Protection gap pressure**: estimates exposure created by uninsured or unfinanced losses, debt stress, hidden exposure, systemic transmission risk, maladaptation risk, affordability pressure, weak coverage, weak pre-arranged finance, and weak equity protection.
- **Risk visibility and governance**: estimates whether risk data, disclosure, mitigation incentives, resilience investment, equity protection, and lower hidden exposure support better decision-making.
- **Resilience-adjusted financial risk**: combines protection-gap pressure, low finance capacity, weak visibility/governance, systemic transmission risk, and debt stress.

## Interpretation

A jurisdiction or portfolio can have sophisticated financial instruments while still carrying high resilience risk if protection gaps, affordability problems, debt stress, hidden exposure, and low resilience investment remain high.

The model is most useful for comparative diagnosis, resilience investment planning, protection-gap analysis, fiscal risk review, and insurance governance.
