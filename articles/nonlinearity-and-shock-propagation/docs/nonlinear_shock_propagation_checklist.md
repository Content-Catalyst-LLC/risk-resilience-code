# Nonlinearity and Shock Propagation Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing systemic risk, cascading failure, infrastructure dependence, climate shocks, cyber disruptions, public-health emergencies, or social-ecological instability.

## Nonlinear Risk Conditions

- Is the system close to a critical threshold?
- Are slow variables moving in a dangerous direction?
- Are there delays between stress accumulation and visible consequence?
- Could a small additional shock trigger rapid change?
- Are average indicators hiding tail risks, extremes, or clustered failures?

## Propagation Pathways

- What physical systems depend on the affected node?
- What information systems, data flows, or communications depend on it?
- What financial, insurance, credit, or budget effects could follow?
- What institutional responsibilities could fail or become unclear?
- What social networks, care systems, or household burdens could be affected?
- What ecological buffers could absorb or amplify the shock?

## Critical Nodes

- Which nodes are highly connected or systemically important?
- Are critical nodes physically exposed to hazards?
- Are they privately owned but publicly essential?
- Are replacement parts, backup systems, or manual fallback procedures available?
- Is there enough visibility into the node's dependencies?

## Containment Capacity

- Are buffers strong enough?
- Is the system modular enough to isolate failure?
- Are credible redundant pathways available?
- Can institutions respond before propagation accelerates?
- Is public communication trusted and accessible?
- Are vulnerable communities protected from absorbing disproportionate harm?

## Governance

- Are dependency maps current?
- Are near misses reviewed?
- Are stress tests conducted across sectors?
- Are thresholds and trigger points tied to action?
- Are after-action reviews used to redesign the system rather than merely restore normal operations?
