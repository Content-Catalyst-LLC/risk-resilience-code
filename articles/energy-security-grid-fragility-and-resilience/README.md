# Energy Security, Grid Fragility, and Resilience

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Energy Security, Grid Fragility, and Resilience**

The workflows model how reliability, adequacy, redundancy, flexibility, distributed capacity, cyber resilience, restoration capacity, critical-load protection, affordability, equity protection, climate exposure, infrastructure aging, fuel dependence, digital fragility, load-growth pressure, and interdependency risk shape energy security and grid resilience.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- Energy security as a systems problem
- Electricity security
- Grid fragility
- Reliability, adequacy, and resilience
- Climate risk and infrastructure stress
- Load growth from electrification, cooling, data centers, artificial intelligence, and industry
- Cyber, digital, and control-system risk
- Fuel supply chains and geopolitical exposure
- Critical infrastructure interdependence
- Cascading failures
- Critical loads and public health
- Energy justice and vulnerable households
- Distributed energy resources
- Microgrids and local fallback capacity
- Grid modernization
- Resilient energy transition

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
energy_security_grid_resilience_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `reliability_index`: higher = stronger routine reliability
- `redundancy_index`: higher = more backup pathways and spare capacity
- `climate_exposure_index`: higher = greater exposure to heat, wildfire, flood, storm, drought, or extreme cold
- `load_growth_pressure_index`: higher = stronger demand growth pressure
