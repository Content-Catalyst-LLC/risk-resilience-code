# Data Dictionary

| Column | Meaning |
|---|---|
| energy_system_name | Name of the grid, utility area, microgrid, regional energy system, or planning unit |
| jurisdiction | Geographic, regulatory, utility, tribal, municipal, state, regional, or national jurisdiction |
| system_type | System category such as bulk grid, distribution network, microgrid, regional grid, utility service area, island system, or critical-load network |
| reliability_index | Routine ability to provide dependable service under expected operating conditions |
| adequacy_index | Whether sufficient resources and infrastructure exist to meet demand |
| redundancy_index | Backup pathways, spare capacity, alternative routes, and non-single-point failure design |
| flexibility_index | Demand response, storage, operational flexibility, and ability to adjust to changing conditions |
| distributed_capacity_index | Distributed energy resources, microgrids, local backup, and community energy systems |
| cyber_resilience_index | Secure digital systems, monitoring, incident response, backup communications, and fallback procedures |
| restoration_capacity_index | Workforce, spare parts, black-start capability, mutual assistance, and restoration planning |
| critical_load_protection_index | Protection for hospitals, water systems, communications, shelters, public safety, and medically vulnerable households |
| affordability_index | Practical affordability of essential energy services |
| equity_protection_index | Protection of low-income, rural, tribal, medically vulnerable, elderly, disabled, and climate-exposed communities |
| climate_exposure_index | Exposure to heat, wildfire, storms, floods, drought, extreme cold, and climate-driven stress |
| infrastructure_aging_index | Asset degradation, maintenance gaps, transformer risk, and outdated equipment |
| fuel_dependence_index | Vulnerability to fuel supply, price, transport, and geopolitical disruption |
| digital_fragility_index | Dependence on communications, data systems, control systems, software, and automation without adequate fallback |
| load_growth_pressure_index | Demand growth from electrification, data centers, artificial intelligence, cooling, industry, and population growth |
| interdependency_risk_index | Risk that energy disruption cascades into water, health, communications, transport, food, finance, or emergency response |
