# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual energy security, grid fragility, and resilience scoring models.

The scores are not objective certifications of grid reliability or energy security. They are diagnostic tools for making reliability, adequacy, redundancy, flexibility, distributed capacity, cyber resilience, restoration capacity, critical-load protection, affordability, equity protection, climate exposure, infrastructure aging, fuel dependence, digital fragility, load-growth pressure, and interdependency risk visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Energy resilience capacity**: estimates the ability of energy systems to preserve critical function, withstand disruption, adapt, and recover.
- **Grid fragility pressure**: estimates pressure from climate exposure, aging infrastructure, fuel dependence, digital fragility, load growth, and interdependency risk.
- **Just energy resilience**: estimates whether affordability, equity protection, critical-load protection, distributed capacity, and restoration capacity are included.
- **Energy-security resilience**: estimates resilience after combining technical capacity, justice capacity, and fragility pressure.
- **Resilience-fragility gap**: compares energy resilience capacity with grid fragility pressure.
- **Cascading energy risk**: describes how energy disruption can propagate into water, health, communications, transport, food, finance, and emergency response.

## Interpretation

A system can be reliable in ordinary conditions but fragile under compound stress. This model helps distinguish routine reliability from broader resilience under climate, cyber, fuel, infrastructure, digital, load-growth, and interdependency stress.

The model is most useful for energy planning, utility resilience review, critical infrastructure planning, climate adaptation, grid modernization, energy justice analysis, and resilient energy-transition strategy.
