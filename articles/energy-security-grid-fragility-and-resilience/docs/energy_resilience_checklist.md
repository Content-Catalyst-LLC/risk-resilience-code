# Energy Security and Grid Resilience Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing energy security, grid fragility, critical infrastructure, utility resilience, climate adaptation, energy justice, microgrids, or energy-transition strategy.

## Reliability, Adequacy, and Resilience

- Is the system reliable under ordinary conditions?
- Is there enough resource adequacy under expected demand?
- Has resilience been tested under extreme, compound, and long-duration events?
- Are climate projections included, not only historical averages?

## Grid Fragility

- Are aging assets, maintenance gaps, and transformer risks visible?
- Are transmission and distribution bottlenecks identified?
- Are fuel dependencies and supply-chain risks assessed?
- Are cyber, digital, and control-system dependencies mapped?
- Are load-growth scenarios from electrification, data centers, AI, cooling, and industry included?

## Cascading Risk

- Which water, health, communications, transport, food, finance, and emergency systems depend on the energy system?
- Which energy assets depend on communications, fuel, roads, water, skilled labor, and spare parts?
- Are critical loads mapped and prioritized?
- Are restoration priorities designed to reduce cascading harm?

## Equity and Public Health

- Which households and communities are most vulnerable to outage?
- Are medically vulnerable customers protected?
- Are cooling, heating, backup power, and resilience hubs planned equitably?
- Are energy affordability and shutoff risk included?

## Resilience Investments

- Are distributed energy resources designed to operate during outages?
- Are microgrids protecting critical facilities and community hubs?
- Are cyber fallback procedures tested?
- Are spare parts, mobile transformers, mutual assistance, and black-start capability available?
- Are resilience claims auditable?
