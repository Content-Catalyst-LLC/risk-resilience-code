# Cross-Sector Coordination and Integrated Resilience Governance

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Cross-Sector Coordination and Integrated Resilience Governance**

The workflows model how cross-sector coordination, dependency visibility, governance integration, data interoperability, public accountability, justice and equity, local capability, adaptive learning, investment alignment, institutional fragmentation, mandate conflict, governance data gaps, maladaptation risk, private-operator opacity, and accountability diffusion shape integrated resilience governance.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- Cross-sector coordination
- Integrated resilience governance
- Policy coherence
- Whole-of-government risk governance
- Critical services and lifeline functions
- Cascading failure and systemic risk
- Infrastructure interdependence
- Data sharing and system visibility
- Multilevel governance
- Public-private-community coordination
- Justice and distribution
- Adaptive governance and institutional learning
- Mandate conflict
- Accountability diffusion
- Coordination failures
- Cross-sector resilience investment

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
integrated_resilience_governance_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `cross_sector_coordination_index`: higher = stronger coordination across agencies, sectors, and systems
- `dependency_visibility_index`: higher = stronger mapping of cross-system dependencies
- `fragmentation_risk_index`: higher = greater institutional fragmentation
- `accountability_diffusion_index`: higher = weaker traceability of responsibility
