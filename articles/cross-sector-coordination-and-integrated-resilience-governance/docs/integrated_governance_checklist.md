# Integrated Resilience Governance Review Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing cross-sector coordination, critical-services continuity, public-sector resilience, regional risk governance, whole-of-government planning, or infrastructure interdependence.

## System Dependencies

- Which critical services must continue?
- What systems support each service?
- What dependencies exist across energy, water, transport, communications, health, food, finance, housing, emergency response, digital systems, and social protection?
- Which dependencies lack redundancy?
- Which dependencies are shared across multiple sectors?

## Coordination

- Are roles and responsibilities clear?
- Are agencies using shared scenarios?
- Are plans tested across sectors?
- Are restoration priorities aligned?
- Are local governments and community organizations included?
- Are private operators part of coordinated governance?

## Data and Visibility

- Are dependency maps current?
- Are vulnerability data disaggregated?
- Can agencies securely share usable data?
- Are data gaps documented?
- Are community knowledge and lived evidence included?

## Justice and Public Accountability

- Who receives protection first?
- Are vulnerable groups visible in planning?
- Are trade-offs public?
- Are communities involved before decisions are finalized?
- Are claims of resilience auditable and contestable?
- Are responsibility and remedy mechanisms clear?

## Learning and Adaptation

- Are after-action reviews system-wide rather than agency-specific?
- Do exercises reveal cross-sector failure pathways?
- Do reviews change budgets, standards, procurement, training, and implementation?
- Are coordination failures tracked and corrected?
