# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual integrated resilience governance scoring models.

The scores are not objective certifications of public-sector readiness. They are diagnostic tools for making coordination capacity, dependency visibility, fragmentation pressure, mandate conflict, accountability diffusion, and data gaps visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Integrated governance capacity**: estimates whether agencies, sectors, operators, communities, and jurisdictions can coordinate around interdependent risks.
- **Coordination fragility pressure**: estimates risk from fragmentation, mandate conflict, weak data, maladaptation, private-operator opacity, and accountability diffusion.
- **Integrated resilience governance gap**: compares integrated coordination capacity against fragility pressure.
- **Dependency visibility**: whether cross-system dependencies are known before disruption.
- **Data interoperability**: whether evidence can move securely, accountably, and usefully across institutions.
- **Public accountability**: whether responsibilities, trade-offs, data, and decisions are transparent and correctable.
- **Local capability**: whether local governments and communities have authority, resources, trust, and practical capacity.
- **Adaptive learning**: whether exercises, disruptions, audits, and community feedback lead to revised practice.

## Interpretation

An agency can perform well within its own mandate while the broader governance system remains fragile.

The model is most useful for cross-sector planning, resilience audits, public-institution stress testing, dependency mapping, critical services analysis, whole-of-government risk governance, and integrated resilience investment review.
