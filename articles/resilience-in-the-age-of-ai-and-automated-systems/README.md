# Resilience in the Age of AI and Automated Systems

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Resilience in the Age of AI and Automated Systems**

The workflows model how AI systems, automation, model reliability, monitoring capacity, human oversight, governance, contestability, fallback capacity, concentration risk, automation dependence, drift risk, opacity, and public accountability shape resilience.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- AI resilience capacity
- Automation fragility
- Model reliability
- Monitoring capacity
- Human oversight
- Governance strength
- Explainability
- Contestability
- Fallback capacity
- Data quality
- Bias management
- Privacy protection
- Cyber resilience
- Concentration risk
- Automation dependence
- Drift risk
- Opacity risk
- Public accountability
- Service criticality

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
ai_resilience_system_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `model_reliability_index`: higher = stronger model reliability
- `monitoring_capacity_index`: higher = stronger post-deployment monitoring
- `oversight_capacity_index`: higher = stronger human oversight
- `concentration_risk_index`: higher = greater concentration risk
- `drift_risk_index`: higher = greater model drift or distribution-shift risk
