# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual AI resilience scoring models.

The scores are not objective measurements of AI safety or resilience. They are diagnostic tools for making implementation and governance assumptions visible.

## Core Concepts

- **AI resilience capacity**: estimates protective capacity created by reliability, monitoring, oversight, governance, explainability, contestability, fallback systems, data quality, bias management, privacy protection, and cyber resilience.
- **Automation fragility**: estimates fragility created by concentration risk, automation dependence, drift risk, opacity risk, weak fallback capacity, weak monitoring, weak oversight, weak contestability, and weak public accountability.
- **Resilience-adjusted AI risk**: combines automation fragility, low AI resilience capacity, service criticality, concentration risk, drift risk, opacity risk, and public accountability gaps.

## Interpretation

An AI system can be technically capable and still create resilience risk when it is opaque, weakly monitored, highly concentrated, poorly contestable, or unsupported by fallback capacity.

The model is most useful for comparative diagnosis, governance review, AI risk management, and scenario discussion.
