# Model Notes

This article uses conceptual planetary boundary and global system risk scoring models.

The scores are not objective certifications of planetary risk. They are diagnostic tools for making boundary transgression, pressure trends, interaction strength, reversibility risk, human-system exposure, monitoring confidence, adaptive capacity, governance quality, justice transition capacity, and policy response visible.

## Core Concepts

- **Planetary pressure score**: estimates the combined stress from boundary transgression, worsening trends, interactions, irreversibility, and human-system exposure.
- **Response capacity score**: estimates the capacity to monitor, govern, adapt, respond, and transition justly.
- **Planetary system risk score**: estimates risk after combining planetary pressure, exposure, irreversibility, and response capacity.
- **Earth-system resilience margin**: compares response capacity against planetary pressure.
- **Boundary interaction risk**: represents reinforcing relationships among climate, biosphere, land, water, oceans, nutrients, pollution, and atmospheric processes.
- **Justice transition capacity**: represents whether transition pathways protect rights, livelihoods, vulnerable communities, and future generations.

## Interpretation

A boundary domain can have high monitoring confidence while still showing severe planetary risk if pressure, transgression, interaction, irreversibility, and human-system exposure are high and response capacity remains weak.

The model is most useful for planetary-health dashboards, sustainability risk analysis, resilience strategy, Earth-system governance review, policy scenario comparison, and integrated environmental risk reporting.
