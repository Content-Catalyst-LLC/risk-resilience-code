# Earth-System Resilience Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing planetary boundaries, global system risk, sustainability strategy, planetary-health dashboards, or transition pathways.

## Boundary Status and Pressure

- Which boundaries are breached, worsening, stabilizing, or recovering?
- Are pressure trends measured, not only current status?
- Are regional expressions of global boundaries visible?
- Are slow variables and delayed effects included?

## Interactions and Cascades

- How does climate change interact with biodiversity, freshwater, land systems, oceans, nutrients, and novel entities?
- Are food, water, energy, health, infrastructure, migration, finance, and governance impacts mapped?
- Are cascading and compounding risks considered?

## Response Capacity

- Are monitoring systems strong enough to detect boundary pressure?
- Are governance institutions able to act across sectors and scales?
- Are policy responses proportional to pressure trends?
- Are adaptive capacity and ecological repair included?

## Justice and Responsibility

- Who contributed most to boundary transgression?
- Who is most exposed to harm?
- Who has capacity to transition?
- Are rights, livelihoods, Indigenous knowledge, community voice, and vulnerable populations protected?

## Resilience Margin

- Is response capacity growing faster than planetary pressure?
- Are interventions reducing pressure across multiple boundaries?
- Are solutions creating hidden trade-offs in other boundary domains?
