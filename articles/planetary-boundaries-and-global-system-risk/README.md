# Planetary Boundaries and Global System Risk

This article directory supports the Risk & Resilience article:

**Planetary Boundaries and Global System Risk**

The workflows model how boundary transgression, pressure trends, interaction strength, reversibility risk, human-system exposure, monitoring confidence, adaptive capacity, governance quality, justice transition capacity, and policy response shape planetary system risk and Earth-system resilience margins.

## Analytical Focus

The article examines:

- Planetary boundaries as an Earth-system resilience framework
- Safe operating space for humanity
- Boundary transgression
- Global system risk
- Cascading ecological and human-system impacts
- Climate change
- Biosphere integrity
- Land-system change
- Freshwater change
- Biogeochemical flows
- Ocean acidification
- Novel entities
- Stratospheric ozone depletion
- Atmospheric aerosol loading
- Food, water, energy, and material systems
- Justice, responsibility, and unequal risk
- Monitoring boundaries and planetary intelligence
- Earth-system resilience

## Suggested Input Dataset

Expected file:

```text
planetary_boundaries_risk_panel.csv
```

Each `*_index` field should be normalized from 0 to 1.

Higher values should mean more of the named property. For example:

- `boundary_transgression_index`: higher = farther beyond safer operating conditions
- `pressure_trend_index`: higher = faster worsening pressure
- `interaction_strength_index`: higher = stronger reinforcing interaction with other boundaries
- `adaptive_capacity_index`: higher = stronger response capacity
- `justice_transition_index`: higher = stronger justice safeguards in transition pathways
